A38. [HIGH] No uniform entity base ledger — id/cost{metal,energy}/mass/health are not a shared
    floor every kind carries.
    src/types/* (unitTypes/combatTypes/locomotionTypes/shotTypes) + the generated blueprint
    schema + rts-sim-wasm/src/lib.rs entity/pool structs. —
    RULE: new "Every entity shares one base ledger" — before it is any kind, every entity carries
    id, a {metal,energy} cost, a mass, and a health pool; a sub-part's cost and health are its own
    line items, so a host's totals are the sum of its pieces, not one opaque number. —
    PROBLEM: cost/mass/health are not carried uniformly across kinds. Turrets and locomotion
    sub-entities do not carry their own cost/health as independent line items; shots carry no
    health at all (see A40). The base is implicit and per-kind rather than one shared contract. —
    FIX: define one shared entity base (id, cost{metal,energy}, mass, health) that every kind
    extends; give turrets and locomotion their own cost+health line items; give shots a health
    field; derive host totals as the sum of piece costs. Put the base in the shared schema so TS
    and Rust cannot drift (the A36 schema-generation path is the place to land it).

A39. [HIGH] Sub-entities are not independently destructible; the host does not degrade per piece.
    rts-sim-wasm/src/lib.rs + src/game/sim (unit/turret/locomotion damage + death paths). —
    RULE: new "Pieces have their own health and die independently" — a turret can explode/detach
    while its unit fights on; a destroyed locomotion drops the unit and stops its movement while
    body+turrets survive; only body death kills the whole assembly. —
    PROBLEM: damage resolves against a single host health pool; mounted turrets and locomotion do
    not take or track damage independently, so you cannot disarm a unit by killing its turret or
    immobilize it by killing its locomotion. —
    FIX: route damage to the specific sub-entity hit; on turret death detach/explode it and keep
    the host alive with that weapon's capability removed; on locomotion death stop movement (no
    pathfind/arrival) but keep body+turrets; cascade to full death only when body health hits
    zero. A piece that DETACHES while still alive (e.g. a turret that survives the host's death
    blast, or one a bomb-unit launches) stays a live, fully-autonomous agent — it keeps acquiring
    and firing on its own until its OWN health hits zero; detachment removes it from the host
    control hierarchy ("Every piece is an agent; the player commands only hosts") but does not end
    its life or agency. (Depends on A38 per-piece health; mirror of A41.)

A40. [MEDIUM] Shots have no health and cannot be shot down; collision is a removal flag, not a
    zero-health detonation.
    src/game/sim/combat/projectileSystem.ts (projectilesToRemove path ~1062/1100/1110/1134),
    src/game/sim/combat/ProjectileCollisionHandler.ts (mid-air detonation ~289-304),
    src/types/shotTypes.ts + src/game/sim/blueprints/shots.json. —
    RULE: new "Shots are destructible bodies too" — every shot has a tiny health pool and
    detonates at zero (normal explosion/splash/submunition path). Contact with ground / another
    shot / any solid body forces health to zero on the spot; FORCE FIELDS ARE THE SOLE EXCEPTION
    (reflect, do not damage). Small-but-real health lets a point-defense turret whittle a round
    down and pop it mid-air. —
    PROBLEM: shots have no health field; collision removal is a separate flag rather than a
    zero-health detonation; shots take no weapon damage, so they cannot be intercepted.
    Force-field reflection already leaves the shot intact (correct), but the rest is not modelled
    as health. —
    FIX: add a tiny health field to the shot; on contact with ground/shot/solid set health=0 and
    run the existing detonation path; route incoming weapon damage to shot health so point-defense
    turrets can shoot shots down; keep force-field reflection as the one non-damaging
    contact. (Pairs with A38 base ledger.)

A41. [MEDIUM] Hosts spawn whole, not built bottom-up piece by piece.
    src/game/sim (factory/fabricator construction + host spawn) + build-site creation. —
    RULE: new "Hosts build from the ground up, one piece at a time" — a unit builds its
    locomotion, then its body, then its turrets; a tower builds its body then its turret(s); a
    building builds its single body. Each piece is the real entity from frame one (carrying its
    final EntityId) in an under-construction state with health ramping from zero, in dependency
    order. —
    PROBLEM: construction produces a single build site that becomes the whole host at once;
    mounted pieces are not built as separate, separately-costed, separately-healthed sub-builds in
    dependency order, so a half-built "chassis on tracks, no gun yet" state does not exist. —
    FIX: build sub-entities as ordered sub-builds (locomotion -> body -> turrets; body -> turret),
    each paying its own cost and ramping its own health, each addressable/destructible mid-build;
    interrupting leaves exactly the pieces already built. (Reverse of A39 per-piece death; depends
    on A38 per-piece cost/health.)

A42. [MEDIUM] Entities do not detonate a death explosion (radius + force + damage) when they die;
    death is removal plus a visual effect, not a physical blast.
    src/game/sim/Simulation.ts (onUnitDeath / deathContexts ~224-230/655-730 — death contexts are
    for VISUAL/directional explosion effects, not gameplay area force), src/game/sim/damage/
    DamageSystem.ts (applyAreaDamage ~952, AreaDamageSource — area damage EXISTS but is driven by
    projectile splash, not entity death), src/game/sim/combat/damageHelpers.ts (explosionRadius is
    a shot/projectile splash field), the generated blueprint schema (explosionRadius lives on
    shots, not as a universal entity field), rts-sim-wasm/src/lib.rs (area damage / impulse). —
    RULE: new "Every death is an explosion" + the death-explosion field in "Every entity shares one
    base ledger" — every entity carries a {radius, force, damage} death blast and detonates it when
    health hits zero; the blast applies outward force (heavier bodies resist more) and damage to
    every entity in radius, friend or foe. Default unit/building blasts are tuned to USUALLY destroy
    their own locomotion and turrets (the physical mechanism behind "killing the body kills the
    assembly"); blueprints may author a deliberately huge blast (e.g. a suicide unit that launches
    its turrets). —
    PROBLEM: a unit's/building's death currently removes the entity and emits a visual explosion
    effect; it does not apply gameplay area force + area damage to surrounding entities, and there
    is no universal per-entity {radius, force, damage} death-explosion field. Splash area-damage
    exists only for projectiles. So deaths neither chain-react nor destroy the dead host's own
    pieces via physics. —
    FIX: add a {radius, force, damage} death explosion to the universal entity base (A38); on any
    entity death detonate it through the existing area-damage kernel (DamageSystem.applyAreaDamage)
    plus the impulse/force path so it pushes and damages everything in radius; tune default unit/
    building blasts to usually destroy their own locomotion+turrets (A39 per-piece health makes that
    a real outcome rather than a no-op); let blueprints override radius/force/damage for bomb-style
    units. Unify shot splash and entity death onto one blast path. (Depends on A38 base ledger +
    A39 per-piece destructibility; pairs with A40 shot detonation.)

A43. [MEDIUM] Shot radii still use an ad-hoc runtime/visual vocabulary instead of the shared
    role-named entity radii.
    src/game/sim/blueprints/blueprintSchema.json (shot runtime/visual radii collisionRadius/
    impactRadius/explosionRadius/damageRadius/projectileBodyRadius ~465-518),
    src/game/sim/shotProfiles.ts, ProjectileRenderer3D.ts, ProjectileCollisionHandler.ts,
    projectile/audio wire context fields. —
    RULE: new "Entity geometry is three radii" + "Every entity shares one base ledger" (A38) —
    geometry is base-ledger: every entity carries exactly three role-named radii — radiusVisual
    (drawn size), radiusHitbox (damage-receiving hurtbox), radiusCollision (body-vs-body no-overlap
    contact) — and the same three names mean the same things on units/towers/buildings/turrets/
    locomotion/shots. The death explosion's own radius stays separate (A42); "push" is reserved for
    a future sustained push-field capability. —
    PROBLEM: units and turrets now use the generated shared radius:{visual, hitbox, collision}
    schema, the TS/WASM bridge names now use visual/hitbox/collision, death-context pushRadius was
    renamed to collisionRadius, and guard/attack/repair/reclaim targeting no longer read the visual
    radius. The remaining drift is shots: shot profiles still expose collisionRadius, damageRadius,
    explosionRadius, and projectileBodyRadius instead of the nested role vocabulary, and splash /
    impact radii still need to be separated from standing-body geometry per A40/A42. —
    FIX: fold shot standing geometry into radius:{visual, hitbox, collision}; map projectileBodyRadius
    to radius.visual and projectile collision to radius.collision; keep splash/explosion radii on
    the shot detonation/death-explosion path rather than in the entity-radius trio; update renderer,
    collision, and wire context names accordingly. (Part of A38 base ledger; touches A40/A42 radii.)

VERIFIED-CLEAN (audited, no action needed — recorded so we don't re-investigate)

(2026-05-30 audit: 21 candidate findings were refuted by independent verifiers
that re-read the live code. The notable ones — things one might assume are gaps
but are not — are folded into the list below.)

- No acceleration on the wire; PREDICT mode is POS/VEL only; no ACC option.
- The four EMA channels (movement pos/vel, rotation pos/vel) are each applied to
  their documented fields. NB: body angularVelocity3 is EMA-corrected but NOT
  integrated into orientation — see A23 (the channel binding exists; the
  integration step does not).
- Travelling projectiles are event-driven (spawn / velocityUpdate / despawn) and
  dead-reckoned — they do NOT ride the movement-position/velocity EMA channels.
- Delta thresholds: position uses absolute error, velocity uses relative; the
  zero-velocity edge is sent; baseline = last value actually sent per recipient.
- Command-upward / snapshot-downward network architecture audited COMPLIANT:
  commands go through the host (authority/sanitize via commandAuthority.ts +
  commandSanitizer.ts), selection is local, snapshots are corrections. (The
  snapshot RATE default is out of band — see A21; and the rate dropdown also
  exposes 32/64/128 options up to the tick rate, refuted as a config-option
  range, not a shipped default.)
- Locomotion arrival control for GROUND, HOVER, and FLYING units correctly uses
  position error + velocity error with thrust saturation for final waypoints
  (compute_arrival_control_thrust in lib.rs ~681-808); final vs intermediate
  waypoints are distinguished (ARRIVAL_FLAG_LAST_ACTION); waypoints are desired
  states, not magnets.
- Gravity integration is universal in the body integrator (lib.rs passes
  authored_az − GRAVITY with no per-body skip branch); homing counter-thrust,
  D-gun terrain-follow, and hover lift are CLEAN — hover fights g·m with a lift
  force, it does NOT skip gravity. Projectile shots now validate a positive
  gravityForceMultiplier; line weapons still use 0 because they are line traces,
  not ballistic bodies.
- Hover lift is an anti-gravity force, not a gravity-skip conditional (verifier
  re-read confirmed; the earlier "hover is a PD pull, not anti-gravity" candidate
  was refuted).
- The host-directed one-per-kind STARTUP THROW IS enforced:
  validateHostDirectedMounts (src/game/sim/blueprints/index.ts:717-733) groups a
  host's mounts by turret kind and requires exactly one hostDirected per present
  kind (zero = orders can't land; ≥2 = ambiguous primary), skipping buildings.
  (The earlier "loader does not enforce" candidate was a misread and is refuted.)
- Level-1 named lock-on exclusions ARE applied in the targeting kernel:
  combat_targeting_level1_mask_excludes (rts-sim-wasm/src/lib.rs ~13000, used at
  13069/13084/13091/13098) filters individual unit/tower/building/turret blueprint
  names AFTER the level-0 family pass, with unit tests
  (level1_body_exclusions_reject_only_matching_blueprints :25975,
  level1_turret_exclusions_filter_individual_mounted_turrets :26046). Level-0
  (friend/enemy + entity family) is also applied. (The earlier "level-1 never
  applied" candidate is refuted.)
- ShotSource provenance is complete and propagated: createTurretShotSource
  (projectileSystem.ts:359-377) populates the full record incl.
  sourceTurretBlueprintId; the "blueprint id can be null" candidate was refuted on
  the merits.
- EntityId is an opaque handle resolved through metadata rows; player/team/host/
  blueprint/storage are NOT packed into the number. EntityMeta generation/alive
  are active: the Rust registry generation-checks refs and the TypeScript mirror
  retains dead rows with alive=false.
- Build sites and factory unit shells are the real product entities from spawn:
  one continuous EntityId from nanoframe to finished structure; no re-id handoff.
- EntityType has Tower as a true peer of Unit/Building; the fabricator is in the
  tower catalog, not a building; pure-infra buildings carry no turrets/lock-on.
- Turrets name one neutral shotId; force-field PANEL and SPHERE both use
  forceField shots (forceFieldPanelShot / forceFieldBarrierShot) that reference
  the shared reflectiveForceField material. Panel geometry lives on the host
  mount, while material behavior/visuals live on the material.
- Host-directed vs fully-autonomous turret mounts are wired correctly: the tag
  lives on the unit/tower MOUNT entry (not the turret blueprint); host lock-on
  overwrites host-directed turrets, fully-autonomous run independently;
  host-directed mounts fall back to autonomous when the host has no lock; the
  fight-stop ratio counts only host-directed mounts.
- Rockets inherit the launch target and never re-acquire; lost target → ballistic.
- Airborne mount invariant (mount.y===0 && mount.z===0, no zResolver) is enforced
  and throws at startup for hover/flying turrets.
- Locomotion visual state (wheel/tread/leg/fan) is computed client-side in the rig
  and does NOT cross the wire.
- Camera anchoring is two orthogonal config axes (anchorPointCameraScreen +
  anchorPointCameraTerrain) resolved by one shared resolver for mouse and touch
  rotate/zoom/pan gestures; all six combinations are reachable from config.
- Sensors are capability sources with fixed radii (NOT derived from WEAPON range).
  (Builder sight IS partly derived from build-reach — a separate, milder coupling;
  see A22.)
- Resource movement: every per-tick stockpile delta funnels through the single
  ResourceMovementSystem credit/debit path (creditStockpile resourceMovement.ts:38,
  debitStockpile :53 are the only mutators of stockpile.curr); one shared render
  rig draws all streams. No side-channel mutates the player pool. (The earlier
  side-channel-bypass candidate was refuted; a producer-income-via-config-scalars
  candidate was also refuted — the doc does not require pylon endpoints authored on
  the host blueprint.)
- Selection panels are gated on entity TYPE capability flags, not per-blueprint
  buttons; mixed-type selections show the action-set intersection plus explicit
  select-only controls for narrowing the active type.
- Factory production is a single repeat-build selection plus one static rally:
  no multi-item build queue, no index cancel command, no waypoint append path,
  and the snapshot DTO carries selectedUnitBlueprintCode + rally.
- Blueprints are pure data: JSON files imported by thin TS loaders (units.ts imports
  units.json, turrets.ts imports turrets.json, etc.); no closures/functions in
  blueprint JSON; config.ts holds zero authoritative tuning literals. Load-time
  validation THROWS with no silent defaults (jsonValidation.ts assertExplicitFields);
  Explicit Absence is honored (required null fields). Single Source Of Truth holds
  (audio refs, JSON-import derivation, cross-config consistency throws). (Config-Is-
  Data audited CLEAN; the TS/Rust blueprint and shot type surfaces are now
  schema-generated.)
- Steady-state tick + renderer per-frame paths use counted loops and reused scratch/
  Sets; no per-frame map/filter/Object.entries/new-Set in hot paths.
- Collision/splash broadphase and spatial partitioning are fully WASM-owned
  (SpatialGrid is a thin wrapper); no full-world steady-state scans in projectile
  collision or area damage. PhysicsEngine3D is LIVE authoritative physics but a
  correct thin WASM orchestrator (NOT a Rust-sim duplicate, NOT dead).
- No banned libs (Phaser/Pixi/Matter/cannon/ammo/rapier) are imported anywhere in
  src/; the only @dimforge entry is a local no-op stub (see D1).
- No legacy/old/_unused dirs or .old/.bak files in src/ or rts-sim-wasm/.
- Client unit prediction motion uses a batched Rust/WASM kernel for target
  extrapolation and rendered entity motion; the old TS single-body wrapper path is
  gone.
- Adaptive terrain mesh build is fully Rust/WASM-owned (C16 closed). One kernel
  `terrain_build_adaptive_mesh` runs the whole host bake — hierarchy refine, 2:1
  balance, conforming T-junction topology, crack-repair loop, vertex-height
  smoothing, triangle neighbor metadata, and the cell->triangle index — sampling the
  same analytic height generator the metal-deposit kernels use. TypeScript is pure
  object assembly (config slice in, TerrainTileMap out); the old TS topology/repair
  code and the granular neighbor/discrepancy/smoothing WASM bridge fallbacks were
  deleted (Delete The Old Path). The 22-value generation-config + 5-stride flat-zone
  packers now live once in terrainGenerationConfig.ts, shared by the mesh baker and
  metalDepositConfig.ts. Cell-index WASM exports stay (client wire-unpack reuses them).
