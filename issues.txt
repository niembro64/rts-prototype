 ISSUES:

1. Oversized orchestration files violate the small-file goal and hide lifecycle
   coupling.
   Evidence:
   - src/components/GameCanvas.vue is still about 3345 lines after extracting
     persisted chrome state to src/components/gameCanvasChromeState.ts,
     client telemetry to src/components/gameCanvasTelemetry.ts, background
     battle lifecycle to src/components/gameCanvasBackgroundBattle.ts,
     client presence/clock to src/components/gameCanvasPresence.ts, and the
     sound-test shortcut to src/components/gameCanvasSoundTest.ts, and
     real-battle timers/listeners/host command forwarding to
     src/components/gameCanvasRealBattleLifecycle.ts, and foreground scene
     wait-interval cleanup to
     src/components/gameCanvasForegroundSceneBinding.ts, and foreground game
     instance/client-view-state create/destroy to
     src/components/gameCanvasForegroundGame.ts, and real-battle terrain/server
     startup helpers to src/components/gameCanvasRealBattleStartup.ts, and
     real-battle foreground startup/scene binding to
     src/components/gameCanvasRealBattleStart.ts, and lobby preview
     restart/re-parenting to src/components/gameCanvasLobbyPreview.ts, and
     NetworkManager callback binding to
     src/components/gameCanvasNetworkCallbacks.ts, and lobby action handlers to
     src/components/gameCanvasLobbyActions.ts, and lobby terrain/settings sync
     to src/components/gameCanvasLobbySettings.ts. It still owns high-level
     real-battle handoff entrypoints and several server/client bar setting
     handlers.
   - src/game/scenes/RtsScene3D.ts is about 2151 lines and owns snapshot intake,
     prediction, audio scheduling, camera, selection, minimap data, renderer
     update ordering, and renderer teardown. See the large update loop at
     src/game/scenes/RtsScene3D.ts:826 and shutdown at
     src/game/scenes/RtsScene3D.ts:2102.
   - Other large split candidates: src/game/network/stateSerializer.ts
     (~1745 lines), src/game/render3d/Render3DEntities.ts (~1573 lines),
     src/game/network/NetworkManager.ts (~1268 lines),
     src/game/network/ClientViewState.ts (~1167 lines).
   Impact:
   - Lifecycle ownership is hard to prove, duplicate helpers reappear, and
     fixes often touch unrelated concerns.
   Remedy:
   - Continue splitting by ownership: GameCanvas composables for server
     lifecycle and real battle handoff; RtsScene3D systems for snapshot
     intake/audio/prediction/render phases; stateSerializer modules by DTO
     family; Render3DEntities into unit/body/turret/building overlay
     renderers.

2. We don't want units in water. We need to find a good solution for jumping units. My initial though is that we predict all landing points of a jump before we do it, and if it is on water (or too near water) we cancel it. This might go along with jumping units only jumping sometimes, only when engaged with a target or when moving. Maybe we have "able to jump" status turned on when it meets any necessary criteria, including next jump wouoldn't jump into water. Jumping should also ignore the ground normal angle, so units can jump up hills (i.e. a jump on a hill won't be in the direction of the normal of the surface, it will be up). Jumps should also happen off of any surface, not just the ground - units, buildings, etc.

3. Beam segments should never just end in the air. Currently we are ending them if they reach a max radius, but that isn't physically correct. We either need to simulate the beam to its logical ending point (including infinity space) or just keep what we have on the simulation, and fake the infinity beam by extending last leg of the beam segments to extend that direction to infinity. Whatever is necessary but doesn't kill performance.

4. Because we have "falling" (jumping) units now, we need to update our ballistics and aiming solvers to account for acceleration as well. Make sure that all instances of aiming and ballistics require 3d position, 3d velocity, and 3d acceleration (can be gravity + other stuff potentially) for both the origin point as well as the target point. This will allow us to have more accurate aiming and ballistics for all turrets against all units / buildings. have that function be atomic and reusable. We may have different things generating acceleration so make sure we don't just hardcode gravity to be used in the function.
