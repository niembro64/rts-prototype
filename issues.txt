SNAP-WIRE-01 - parent: snapshot payloads exceed the current per-client
               internet budget at small battle sizes.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  the remaining FULLSNAP static sections. FULLSNAP bootstrap moved from
  961,963 B to 291,265 B. The `buildability` section moved from
  561,920 B to 11,933 B via exact run-length triples over flag/level
  cell pairs, and the `terrain` section moved from 357,285 B to
  236,331 B via compact binary numeric slabs. Static decode smoke

  FULLSNAP bootstrap is now under the 1 MiB target at both tested caps

Why it matters:
Snapshot bandwidth is per remote client and burns host upstream first.
Even when the 200-unit delta stream is under target and the high-count

DIFFSNAP stream. The 1,000 / 5,000-unit rows still exceed the normal
internet delta budget by several multiples.

Fix direction:
Do child items as separate, measurable PRs. FULLSNAP/static sections are
not the active blocker; the next work is SNAP-WIRE-01A.

budget the next largest delta sections: projectile/beam bursts,
minimap rows, and remaining audio-event bursts.

TYPESCRIPT IN THE HOT PATH (TS-WASM-01 family)

The "TypeScript Coordinates, WASM Computes" section of
design_philosophy.html is explicit: per-unit, per-projectile, and
per-frame loops must live in WebAssembly (compiled from Rust), not in
TypeScript. Several per-unit loops still run in TS today. None are
catastrophic at 200 units, but they all compound at the 5,000-unit
design target.

==============================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TS-WASM-01 - parent: per-entity hot loops still execute in
             TypeScript when their kernels should live in Rust.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- src/game/sim/Simulation.ts (1,657 LOC) is still the top-level tick
  driver. Although most heavy kernels delegate to WASM, several
  per-unit loops are TypeScript:
    - applyArrivalThrust (Simulation.ts:1214-1293) — PD controller
      that computes locomotion thrust per unit per tick.
    - updateUnitGroundNormal (src/game/sim/unitGroundNormal.ts) —
      per-unit ground-normal EMA, called every tick.
    - UnitSuspensionSystem.update (src/game/sim/unitSuspension.ts) —
      per-unit suspension spring integration, called every tick.
    - UnitForceSystem.applyForces (src/game/server/UnitForceSystem.ts)
      — per-unit force gathering, called every tick from
      GameServer.tick() (GameServer.ts:392 region).
- src/game/server/PhysicsEngine3D.ts (915 LOC) and
  src/game/server/UnitForceSystem.ts (699 LOC) are still instantiated
  in GameServer.ts:192-193 and called every tick. The integration
  itself now runs in Rust kernels, but the per-unit force-selection
  loop, suspension state, and physics-body bookkeeping all live in TS.
- Client prediction has a similar shape: ClientPredictionStepper,
  ClientUnitPrediction, and ClientProjectilePrediction iterate over
  predicted entities per render frame in TS, even though they call
  into shared kernels (advanceUnitMotionPhysicsMutable,
  solveKinematicIntercept, computeHomingThrust).
- Snapshot wire packing runs per-snapshot loops in TS
  (snapshotEntityWirePack.ts, snapshotProjectileWirePack.ts,
  snapshotMinimapWirePack.ts, stateSerializer*.ts). At 5,000 entities
  per 10 SPS snapshot, this is on the edge of the budget the
  philosophy reserves for "large serialization loops".

Why it matters:
The philosophy lists per-unit update loops, physics integration and
collision detection, and large serialization loops explicitly in the
"What TypeScript must never own in hot paths" section. The current
split keeps the kernels in Rust but the per-unit iteration in JS.
At 5,000 active units, an N-per-tick TS loop is exactly the scaling
shape the design rules out.

Fix direction:
Do child items as separate, measurable PRs. Order matters: move the
larger, more frequent loops first (arrival control) and use the
patterns established to migrate the smaller EMA/spring loops.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TS-WASM-01A - child: move applyArrivalThrust into a Rust batch
              kernel so locomotion PD-control runs in WASM.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- Simulation.ts:1214-1293 currently computes per-unit:
    - target-velocity from waypoint + arrival shape,
    - position error (waypoint - body.pos),
    - velocity error (target - body.vel),
    - thrust = positionGain · pos_err - dampingGain · body.vel,
    - clamp/saturate against authored thrust limits,
    - write back to body's force accumulator via
      setUnitMovementAcceleration.
- The Rust crate already exposes the physics pool, force accumulator,
  and unit motion helpers (advance_unit_motion in lib.rs around 565).
  An arrival-control kernel can sit alongside them and consume the
  same packed pool storage.

Fix direction:
Land a Rust kernel `arrival_control_step_batch(...)` that reads the
authored thrust/mass profile, body kinematics, and current waypoint
from packed slabs and writes the per-tick thrust into the existing
force accumulator. Delete applyArrivalThrust from Simulation.ts in
the same change (Delete The Old Path). Verify gameplay parity at
N=200 and check 5,000-unit tick budget; the PD math is unchanged,
only the iteration moves.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TS-WASM-01B - child: move updateUnitGroundNormal EMA into Rust.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- src/game/sim/unitGroundNormal.ts iterates over every unit each tick
  and EMA-blends a smoothed surface normal. It is called every tick
  from Simulation.update().
- The same normal feeds both server-side chassis tilt and renderer
  ground clamping, so the normal is read on every visual frame too.
  Moving the EMA into WASM keeps a single source of truth for the
  surface normal and removes a per-unit JS loop.

Fix direction:
Add a Rust kernel that EMA-blends the per-unit ground normal against
the smoothed terrain sample. Expose the smoothed values via the same
typed-array pointer mechanism the other pools use. Delete the TS
implementation; the renderer reads the smoothed normal through the
existing accessor.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TS-WASM-01C - child: move UnitSuspensionSystem per-unit spring into
              Rust (or scope it strictly client-side visual).
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- src/game/sim/unitSuspension.ts +
  src/game/server/UnitSuspensionSystem.ts run a per-unit spring
  integrator every tick from GameServer.tick().
- The suspension state is decorative chassis bob; per the
  "Locomotion Visuals Are Frontend" philosophy section, it should
  live on the client renderer or in the Rust sim, never as a TS
  per-unit loop on the host.

Fix direction:
Decide whether the suspension model is gameplay-affecting (move to
Rust kernel, on the wire) or purely visual (move to client renderer,
off the wire entirely). Either way, the TS per-unit loop on the host
should go away.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TS-WASM-01D - child: move snapshot wire-pack and serializer loops
              into Rust now that the encoded shape is stable.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- src/game/network/snapshotEntityWirePack.ts (1,100+ LOC),
  snapshotProjectileWirePack.ts, snapshotMinimapWirePack.ts,
  snapshotAudioWirePack.ts, snapshotStaticWirePack.ts, and the
  stateSerializer*.ts files all run per-snapshot loops over the live
  entity/projectile/minimap/audio sets in TS.
- snapshotRustWireEncoder.ts already wraps a Rust encoder for the
  outer frame but still reads from the TS serializer functions, so
  Rust never owns the inner per-entity loop.
- The 2026-05-22 entity packed-row work shows the wire shape is
  stable enough to land in Rust now (V1-V6 versions are append-only
  and the encoder is bit-deterministic).

Fix direction:
Migrate the per-entity / per-projectile / per-minimap / per-audio
emit loops into the Rust encoder. Keep the JS wrappers only as
thin call-into-WASM shells. This also cuts the JS/WASM boundary
crossings the philosophy warns about: today every snapshot copies
entity slab values from WASM into JS arrays just to be re-packed
into a wire buffer.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TS-WASM-01E - child: client prediction step body is still a per-unit
              JS loop; move the inner step into WASM and let the JS
              shell just drive cadence.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- src/game/network/ClientPredictionStepper.ts,
  ClientUnitPrediction.ts, ClientProjectilePrediction.ts all iterate
  over predicted entities per render frame and call shared kernels
  for the math.
- The philosophy explicitly assigns "client prediction integrator"
  to the WASM layer in the "What TypeScript must never own in hot
  paths" list. The shared kernels are already there; only the
  iteration is still in JS.

Fix direction:
Add a Rust `client_predict_step(dt)` entry point that walks the
predicted-entity slab in linear memory and applies the same EMA /
integration the JS does today. Delete the JS per-entity loop; the
JS layer becomes "set dt, call WASM, sample for render."

EXPLICIT ABSENCE (ABSENCE-01 family)

The "Explicit Absence" section of design_philosophy.html bans `?:`
property declarations and `?.` access inside our own simulation,
server, prediction, networking, and entity-state modules. Optional
chaining usage is already at 0 in the in-scope tree, but optional
property declarations still appear in core types.

==============================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ABSENCE-01 - parent: 308 `?:` declarations remain across in-scope
             modules; 221 of them are in two files.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- src/types/network.ts: 135 `?:` declarations. This file defines the
  NetworkServerSnapshotEntity DTO shape and dozens of inner
  unit/building/turret sub-shapes (pos, unit, building, projectile,
  audio, minimap, etc). The DTOs are pooled — the same object is
  reused across snapshots — so optional fields carry zombie values
  between snapshots unless every release path explicitly resets them.
- src/types/sim.ts: 86 `?:` declarations. This is the core
  entity-state shape file. Optional component slots (`body?`,
  `unit?`, `building?`, `combat?`, `projectile?`, etc.) are the most
  common pattern, and the philosophy is explicit that "field is
  semantically inapplicable to this kind of entity is NOT an
  optional field. It is a discriminated union. Express it that way."
- 87 other `?:` declarations spread across src/game/sim/*, including:
    - Simulation.ts (10): onUnitDeath?, onUnitSpawn?, onBuildingDeath?,
      onSimEvent?, onGameOver? plus a few method param defaults.
    - WorldState.ts (6): RemovedSnapshotEntity.playerId?,
      onEntityRemoving?, etc.
    - snapshotWireTypes.ts (5): audioEvents?, minimapEntities?,
      projectiles?, terrain?, buildability?.
    - combat/combatUtils.ts (10), combat/aimSolver.ts (10),
      buildPlacementValidation.ts (8), combat/damageHelpers.ts (7).
- Sentinel violations (number|null in hot loops, where the
  philosophy prescribes numeric sentinels like `targetId = -1`):
    - src/game/sim/combat/targetingInputStamping.ts:124 — `targetId:
      EntityId | null` on CombatTargetingTurretFsmOut, stamped once
      per turret per frame.
    - src/types/sim.ts:623 — Builder.currentBuildTarget: EntityId |
      null.
    - src/game/sim/damage/DamageSystem.ts:164 — closestEntityId:
      EntityId | null inside a damage-sphere search.
- Transform shape variance: src/types/sim.ts:89-90 declares cached
  `rotCos?` and `rotSin?` on the Transform. Sometimes-populated
  cached fields are exactly the V8 hidden-class deoptimization the
  philosophy calls out.

Why it matters:
"Pooled objects carry zombie fields between users when those fields
are T? rather than T | null with an explicit reset on release."
DTOs in src/types/network.ts are pooled and flow through every
snapshot. Optional-field zombies are exactly the "seemingly ready
when actually not" bug class the philosophy is trying to retire.

Fix direction:
Address as child PRs, file by file. The migration is intentionally
gradual ("every file we touch should leave with fewer optional
properties..."), but two files concentrate most of the risk and
should be the first scheduled passes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ABSENCE-01A - child: convert pooled DTO optional fields in
              src/types/network.ts to T | null with explicit reset.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- NetworkServerSnapshotEntity carries 50+ optional sub-fields across
  pos, unit, building, projectile, combat, audio sub-shapes. The DTO
  is pooled via src/game/network/snapshotPool.ts; the release path
  must guarantee every optional slot is cleared.
- src/game/network/unitSnapshotFields.ts already has clearNetwork*
  helpers; some optional fields may not be reset there. Audit each.

Fix direction:
File-scoped pass. Change each `field?: T` to `field: T | null = null`
with an explicit guard at every reader. Add reset calls in
clearNetwork* / snapshotPool release helpers for any field newly
on the must-null list. Keep the wire encoding unchanged.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ABSENCE-01B - child: convert entity-state component slots in
              src/types/sim.ts to discriminated unions or T | null.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- Entity carries `body?`, `unit?`, `building?`, `combat?`,
  `projectile?`, etc. Per philosophy these should be a discriminated
  union, not optional fields.
- Transform.rotCos?/rotSin? cache fields on src/types/sim.ts:89-90
  are the kind of "sometimes populated" pattern that deoptimizes V8
  hidden classes per philosophy.

Fix direction:
Either model Entity as a discriminated union keyed by `kind`
('unit' | 'building' | 'projectile' | …), or settle for `T | null`
plus explicit guards at the readers. For the rotation cache, either
always populate the cache (uniform shape) or move the cache to a
separate map keyed by entity ID.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ABSENCE-01C - child: swap number|null target IDs for numeric
              sentinels in hot-loop types.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- targetingInputStamping.ts:124 stamps `targetId: EntityId | null`
  for every armed turret every frame.
- Builder.currentBuildTarget (sim.ts:623) and
  closestEntityId (DamageSystem.ts:164) carry the same shape.
- Other hot-loop sites in combat targeting and projectile homing
  use `proj.homingTargetId !== undefined` checks (projectileSystem.ts
  :788, :795) — same anti-pattern in `T | undefined` form.

Fix direction:
Use -1 as the unambiguous "no target" sentinel for entity IDs in hot
loops. Update readers from `x === null` / `x === undefined` to
`x === -1`. Document the sentinel choice once, in a shared comment
or type.

CONFIG IS DATA, NOT CODE (BLUEPRINT-01 family)

The "Config Is Data, Not Code" section of design_philosophy.html
requires blueprints, tuning tables, and authored content in JSON
validated against a shared schema; the TS-to-Rust migration depends
on Rust being able to read the data directly. Today only one constants
file crosses that bridge.

==============================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BLUEPRINT-01 - parent: the Rust sim cannot consume the blueprint
               JSON files; types are hand-maintained on both sides.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- rts-sim-wasm/build.rs only consumes
  src/sharedSimConstants.json (universal tuning constants such as
  GRAVITY). It generates a single Rust constants file.
- The blueprint JSON files (src/game/sim/blueprints/units.json,
  buildings.json, turrets.json, shots.json, locomotion.json,
  unitRoster.json, fallbacks.json) are not consumed by Rust. There
  are no include_str! or serde_json calls in
  rts-sim-wasm/src/lib.rs that load these.
- Type definitions are hand-maintained in TypeScript only
  (src/types/blueprints.ts, ~503 LOC). Rust types are defined
  separately inside lib.rs without a generated bridge.
- 17 top-level config pairs in src/ have both `fooConfig.json` and
  `fooConfig.ts`; 16 other configs are JSON-only. The .ts wrapper
  pattern is inconsistent — the data lives in JSON in both cases,
  but coverage of the typed-import wrapper is uneven.

Why it matters:
The TS-to-Rust migration cannot finish while blueprints live as
TypeScript-readable-only data. Hand-maintained parallel types are
the canonical drift surface: a field added to TS today silently
disagrees with the Rust struct tomorrow.

Fix direction:
Address as child PRs. Order: schema first, then Rust consumption,
then code-generation of types from the schema.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BLUEPRINT-01A - child: write a single schema (JSON Schema or a
                custom validated format) for each blueprint kind and
                land a build-time generator for both TS types and
                Rust structs.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- src/types/blueprints.ts is hand-edited. Rust equivalents (where
  they exist) are hand-edited separately. There is no `schemas/`
  directory at the repo root.

Fix direction:
Add `schemas/blueprints/*.schema.json` (or equivalent). Wire a
build step that generates `src/types/blueprints.generated.ts` and
the Rust counterpart from each schema. Existing hand-edited types
are deleted in the same change once parity is verified
(Delete The Old Path).

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BLUEPRINT-01B - child: have rts-sim-wasm consume the blueprint JSON
                files directly (at build time via include_str! or
                at runtime via a single dump-from-host frame).
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- Rust today knows nothing about units, turrets, shots, buildings,
  or locomotion; all of that data lives in TS data structures
  derived from JSON.
- rts-sim-wasm/build.rs already demonstrates the include-and-generate
  pattern for sharedSimConstants.json.

Fix direction:
Extend build.rs to include the blueprint JSON files and emit
generated Rust constants for each blueprint table, keyed by the
generated structs from BLUEPRINT-01A. Migrate hot-path lookups in
lib.rs to read from those tables instead of receiving blueprint
parameters by argument from JS.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BLUEPRINT-01C - child: decide whether to keep, complete, or drop
                the inconsistent .ts wrapper layer over the
                top-level *Config.json files.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- 17 of 33 top-level configs in src/ have both .json and .ts; the
  other 16 are JSON-only. The .ts wrappers are mostly trivial
  re-exports (`export const X = config.x;`).
- colorsConfig.ts has real logic (CSS hex → RGB tuples). That kind
  of wrapper has clear value.
- Trivial re-export wrappers do not.

Fix direction:
Either standardize: every top-level config gets a generated
typed wrapper (script-generated, not hand-maintained), or none do
and all callers import the JSON directly with `import type`
declarations. Don't ship both shapes side by side.

WIRE-PATH HOT ALLOCATIONS (WIRE-PERF-01 family)

The "Browser Performance Rules" and "Hot paths are data-oriented"
sections require "Reuse arrays by setting length = 0" and "Pool
snapshot DTOs, event objects, scratch arrays, and temporary buffers."
Several snapshot pack/unpack call sites still allocate fresh arrays
per snapshot.

==============================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WIRE-PERF-01 - parent: snapshot pack/unpack helpers allocate fresh
               arrays per snapshot in TS rather than reusing pools.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- The encode / DP-02 measurement path in
  src/game/network/snapshotProjectileWirePack.ts now leases pooled
  numeric arrays for the packed projectile spawns, despawns, velocity
  updates, beam updates, and beam-point slabs via
  src/game/network/snapshotPooledScratch.ts. Remaining projectile
  decode arrays are retained snapshot DTO outputs and should not be
  scratch-pooled until snapshot ownership/lifetime is changed.
- src/game/network/snapshotEntityWirePack.ts still allocates arrays in
  the movement/turret output allocation, grouped chunk, and nested
  detail decode paths.
- src/game/network/snapshotMinimapWirePack.ts V2 removed the
  six-number packed row array from the pack path, but still allocates
  transient group/chunk arrays while packing and a DTO array while
  unpacking.
- These run on the host per snapshot (5-10 Hz) per remote client,
  so the allocation pressure scales with N_entities × N_clients ×
  SPS. At 5,000 entities the allocator gets a steady drumbeat of
  short-lived arrays.

Why it matters:
GC pressure on the host directly translates into snapshot jitter
and tick-budget variance. The philosophy is explicit: "Do not
allocate in per-unit, per-projectile, per-turret, per-cell, or
per-frame loops unless the allocation is proven irrelevant." 5-10
Hz over 5,000 entities is not "proven irrelevant."

Fix direction:
Add per-section pooled scratch arrays in
src/game/network/snapshotPooledScratch.ts (the pool already exists
for other paths). Each pack/unpack uses `arr.length = 0` instead
of `new Array(...)`. Children below split per-file.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WIRE-PERF-01B - child: pool the packed entity sub-slabs in
                snapshotEntityWirePack.ts.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Fix direction:
Apply the same pooled scratch shape to the entity wire pack
allocations. Coordinate with TS-WASM-01D so that the migration to
a Rust encoder doesn't immediately invalidate the pool; pooling
buys time and is a low-risk intermediate.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WIRE-PERF-01C - child: pool the remaining minimap pack/unpack scratch in
                snapshotMinimapWirePack.ts.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Fix direction:
Pool or eliminate the remaining transient group/chunk arrays in the V2
pack path where practical, and decide whether decode output can safely
reuse a DTO pool without corrupting retained snapshot references.

MISCELLANEOUS WIRE / RENDERER / DIAGNOSTIC ITEMS

==============================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RENDER-WEBGPU-01 - Three.js → raw WebGPU migration is unstarted.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- package.json still depends on `three` 0.184.0.
- src/game/render3d/ has 86 files, all built around THREE.* APIs.
- A repo-wide grep for `WebGPU`, `WGSL`, `GPUDevice`, or
  `gpu.requestAdapter()` returns zero matches. The migration that
  the philosophy frames as "the largest example" of replacing
  scaffolding has not begun.
- The "Engine Ownership: No Libraries, In-House Rust" section
  treats Three.js as legacy scaffolding and reserves the end-state
  for raw WebGPU + WGSL.

Why it matters:
This is the biggest outstanding migration in the project, and
keeping it visible as a tracked issue ensures it doesn't get
forgotten beneath smaller items. Replacing Three.js is not
something to start with a single PR; it is a multi-month effort
that should start with a bounded slice (e.g. ground plane,
instanced units, terrain) and migrate piecemeal — but in the
philosophy's own framing, until the old path is gone, the
migration is not done.

Fix direction:
Out of scope for any single child here. The right first step is
to land a WebGPU initialization layer in src/game/render3d/ that
coexists with Three.js, port the simplest renderer (likely
TerrainTileRenderer3D or a primitive ground pass) to it, and
delete the Three.js path for that one renderer in the same
change. Iterate. Don't start a parallel renderer tree without a
deletion plan; the philosophy is explicit that two renderer
shapes interfere even when only one is supposed to be active.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RENDER-PERUNIT-01 - per-entity render-list updates in TS at
                    per-frame cadence.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- src/game/render3d/Render3DEntities.ts iterates over visible
  entities every frame (updateUnits / updateUnitMeshes around
  lines 426+/439+) and updates per-unit pose, turret pose, barrel
  spin, and material color in JS.
- This is the "render-list generation for thousands of objects"
  shape called out in the TS-must-never-own-this list.

Why it matters:
The Three.js scene-graph traversal already has its own per-object
cost; adding a per-unit JS update on top of it compounds the
language-overhead penalty the philosophy is trying to avoid at
scale.

Fix direction:
Tightly coupled to RENDER-WEBGPU-01. Migrating to instanced
WebGPU rendering replaces per-unit JS pose writes with batched
GPU buffer uploads sourced directly from WASM linear memory. Until
then, the best intermediate is to ensure the per-frame JS pass is
strictly batched (no per-unit allocation, no Object.assign, no
.map / .filter / .forEach in the inner loop).

FOG OF WAR CONCEPT REVIEW

==============================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FOW-CONCEPT-01 - the tactical shroud is disabled, so fog of war has no
                 readable explored/unexplored/live-vision surface.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- design_philosophy.html says fog of war and fog filtering are
  authoritative simulation responsibilities, while snapshots should ship
  recipient-aware fog/minimap deltas and clients should present the
  corrected local view.
- src/game/scenes/helpers/RtsScene3DRenderPhase.ts:168-184 calls
  FogOfWarShroudRenderer3D.update(..., false, ...), permanently hiding
  the three-state shroud. The comment says the in-bounds shroud plane
  created an ugly hard edge against the off-map shelf/water, so the
  visual shroud was disabled.
- src/game/render3d/FogOfWarShroudRenderer3D.ts already implements the
  concept players need: unexplored, explored-dark, and currently visible.
  But because it is disabled, the player cannot read what has been
  scouted from the main view.
- src/game/render3d/FogOfWarFog3D.ts still runs when fog is enabled, but
  it spawns decorative soft fog spheres outside current local vision. It
  does not cover all unseen space, does not preserve explored history,
  and does not communicate the actual tactical visibility boundary.

Why it matters:
Server-side filtering can prevent information leaks, but it does not
solve the player-facing RTS problem: the commander needs to know what is
currently visible, what was explored before, and what remains unknown.
The current active visual is ambience, not an information layer.

Fix direction:
Re-enable a strategic shroud after redesigning it so it covers the whole
readable battlefield consistently, including the off-map shelf/horizon
case that caused the current seam. Prefer a terrain/material/WebGPU
overlay or terrain-shader integration over a flat in-bounds plane if
that is what removes the seam. Keep FogOfWarFog3D as optional ambience
only; it should not be the primary fog-of-war read.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FOW-CONCEPT-02 - visual shroud reveal semantics do not match
                 authoritative full-vision semantics.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- src/game/network/stateSerializerVisibility.ts separates full vision
  from radar: full vision sends entities in the main snapshot, while
  radar only reveals minimap blips.
- Full entity visibility in SnapshotVisibility.isEntityVisible() checks
  terrain line of sight through hasTerrainLineOfSight().
- src/game/sim/shroudBitmap.ts uses canEntityProvideVision() plus a
  radius-only markCircle() pass. canEntityProvideVision() explicitly
  includes radar, and the shroud pass does not apply terrain line of
  sight.
- src/game/render3d/FogOfWarShroudRenderer3D.ts and
  src/game/render3d/FogOfWarFog3D.ts collect the same
  canEntityProvideVision()/getEntityVisionRadius() sources, so radar
  and 2D radius coverage visually clear the shroud like optical sight.

Why it matters:
The player can be shown terrain as currently visible or explored even
where the host would still hide enemy entity detail behind a ridge, or
where the only source is radar that should communicate "sensor contact"
rather than true sight. That weakens the fog concept because the visual
language and the authoritative information contract disagree.

Fix direction:
Define separate visibility layers and render them separately:
full optical vision should use the same host-side source classification
and line-of-sight mask as entity visibility; radar should produce a
distinct sensor/minimap treatment; exploration history should be clear
about whether it means "physically seen" or "sensor-covered." The
server should ship or expose compact masks/source packets for those
layers instead of the client re-deriving a different approximation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FOW-CONCEPT-03 - live team vision is not represented by the client-side
                 fog renderers.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- SnapshotVisibility builds a recipient view mask from the recipient
  plus allies, and buildRecipientShroudView() ORs allied shroud history
  into the keyframe payload.
- FogOfWarShroudRenderer3D.collectSources() only reads
  clientViewState.getUnitsByPlayer(localPlayerId) and
  getBuildingsByPlayer(localPlayerId), then adds server-filtered scan
  pulses.
- FogOfWarFog3D.collectSources() has the same local-player-only source
  collection.

Why it matters:
The host correctly lets allied vision affect filtering and shroud
history, but the live visual clear around moving allied units/buildings
is missing on the client. In team play, the main view can receive allied
information while the fog presentation still looks like only the local
seat has eyes.

Fix direction:
Make live fog presentation consume the same team-merged visibility
contract as the snapshot filter. Either ship compact team-visible masks
from the host, or ship a filtered team vision-source packet that includes
only sources the recipient is allowed to know. Do not infer team vision
by scanning arbitrary client entities unless that source set is already
authoritative and recipient-safe.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FOW-CONCEPT-04 - fog visibility ignores force/material sight blockers
                 even though the design treats material LOS as a shared
                 contract.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- design_philosophy.html says surface materials are shape-independent
  contracts and line-of-sight queries should treat every shape carrying
  the same material the same way.
- src/game/sim/combat/lineOfSight.ts has force-field and combat LOS
  helpers that account for force-field/mirror obstruction in combat
  targeting.
- src/game/network/stateSerializerVisibility.ts uses only
  hasTerrainLineOfSight() for fog-of-war entity visibility.
- src/game/sim/terrain/terrainLineOfSight.ts explicitly documents that
  the helper is terrain-only for fog-of-war visibility.

Why it matters:
If mirror/force-field material is meant to block sight, then fog vision
is currently a separate, weaker LOS policy than combat targeting. That
creates a conceptual split: the same material can block a turret's view
but not the player's fog-of-war visibility.

Fix direction:
Decide the rule explicitly. If fog vision should ignore force/material
occlusion, document that exception in the design philosophy and code. If
it should obey the material contract, route fog visibility through a
shared LOS policy that includes terrain plus the relevant material
blockers, with a compact kernel suitable for per-recipient visibility.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FOW-CONCEPT-05 - fog-of-war calculation still lives in TypeScript hot
                 paths instead of the Rust/WASM engine.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- design_philosophy.html assigns visibility and fog to the Rust/WASM
  engine, and explicitly lists pathfinding and fog-of-war calculation
  among the hot paths TypeScript must not own.
- SnapshotVisibility in src/game/network/stateSerializerVisibility.ts
  builds per-recipient source pools and spatial hashes in TypeScript.
- updateShroudBitmaps() in src/game/sim/shroudBitmap.ts walks unit and
  building source lists and marks bitmap circles in TypeScript.
- The active visual fog paths are Three.js/canvas systems in
  FogOfWarShroudRenderer3D.ts and FogOfWarFog3D.ts.

Why it matters:
The present shape is acceptable for prototype validation, but it is not
the end-state architecture described by the philosophy. At 5,000 units,
recipient-specific visibility, shroud masks, minimap contacts, and
projectile/audio gates are exactly the kind of repeated numeric work
that should be batched in WASM and handed to TS as compact results.

Fix direction:
Create a FOW-specific child of the TS-WASM migration: move source
classification, team mask merge, visibility masks, shroud bitmap update,
and recipient query kernels into Rust/WASM over packed entity/source
slabs. TypeScript should orchestrate snapshot routing and rendering, not
own the visibility inner loops.
