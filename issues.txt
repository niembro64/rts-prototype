------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SNAP-WIRE-01 - parent: snapshot payloads exceed the current per-client
               internet budget at small battle sizes.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- The latest 2026-05-22 fixed-point capture in
  docs/dp-02-snapshot-wire-instrumentation.md shows the roughly
  200-unit DIFFSNAP stream back inside budget: 52,554 B avg and
  114,475 B hi, against the 64 KiB avg / 128 KiB hi targets.
- The 2026-05-22 audio packed-row capture in the same doc compares the
  same snapshots through the previous Rust object-audio encoding and
  the new packed-audio wire path. DIFFSNAP avg moved from 63,633 B to
  50,212 B, while audioEvents moved from 16,136 B avg / 31,942 B hi to
  2,715 B avg / 5,186 B hi. Packed decode had zero event-count
  mismatches across 103 captured snapshots.
- The 2026-05-22 minimap/projectile packed-row capture in the same doc
  compares the previous Rust-backed audio-packed / object-minimap /
  object-projectile path against the new packed row wire path.
  DIFFSNAP avg moved from 58,922 B to 49,711 B, and hi moved from
  122,910 B to 98,067 B across 180 captured delta snapshots. The
  minimapEntities section moved from 4,343 B avg to 1,132 B avg, and
  projectiles moved from 9,397 B avg to 3,397 B avg. Packed decode had
  zero minimap/projectile count mismatches across 181 captured
  snapshots.
- The 2026-05-22 entity packed-row code-path lands per-entity flag/row
  packing for the `entities` top-level key. A synthetic 170-entity
  delta-shaped sample (100 pos+vel-only units, 50 keyframe buildings,
  20 hover units with orientation + angular velocity) measured raw
  msgpack at 17,148 B vs packed msgpack at 4,194 B (75.5% reduction)
  via the offline parity test. A 5-entity representative sample
  measured raw at 822 B vs packed at 198 B (75.9% reduction). Live
  2026-05-22 PLAYER CLIENT capture against the same 5-player demo /
  cap 243 / 32 SPS harness measured DIFFSNAP at 13,664 B avg /
  27,054 B hi across 445 deltas, and FULLSNAP bootstrap at 961,963 B.
  The previous audio + minimap + projectile packed-row capture measured
  49,711 B DIFFSNAP avg / 98,067 B hi and 1,069,273 B FULLSNAP
  bootstrap.
- The 2026-05-22 static terrain/buildability packed-row capture attacks
  the remaining FULLSNAP static sections. FULLSNAP bootstrap moved from
  961,963 B to 291,265 B. The `buildability` section moved from
  561,920 B to 11,933 B via exact run-length triples over flag/level
  cell pairs, and the `terrain` section moved from 357,285 B to
  236,331 B via compact binary numeric slabs. Static decode smoke
  passed: buildability flags/levels and all terrain integer arrays
  round-tripped exactly; Float32 terrain coord/height drift stayed
  below 0.0005 world units. DIFFSNAP stayed effectively unchanged
  at 13,803 B avg / 28,072 B hi across 441 deltas.
- After audio, minimap, projectile, entity, terrain, and buildability
  packing, the cap-243 harness is well inside both the 64 KiB DS avg /
  128 KiB DS hi and the 1 MiB FS targets.
- The 2026-05-22 high-count PLAYER CLIENT matrix in
  docs/dp-02-snapshot-wire-instrumentation.md proves the next limit:
  FULLSNAP bootstrap is now under the 1 MiB target at both tested caps
  (about 389-392 KiB at cap 1,000 and 918-923 KiB at cap 5,000), but
  DIFFSNAP is still over budget. The 1,000-cap rows measured
  159,039-211,790 B avg / 218,564-244,394 B hi. The 5,000-cap rows
  measured 671,714-770,596 B avg / 841,268-859,546 B hi, and the host
  only produced about 1.2-1.3 encoded snapshots/sec at that scale in
  the headless harness.
- The 2026-05-22 follow-up Vite SSR probe in the same doc adds the
  version-3 unit turret slab and changes continuous beam/force-field
  audio from every-tick start/stop streaming to transition events plus
  a 60-tick active refresh. The 1,000-cap / 10 SPS row is now inside
  target at 43,519 B avg / 83,281 B hi across 83 delta samples. The
  5,000-cap / 10 SPS row is still over budget at 221,055 B avg /
  322,977 B hi across 13 delta samples. At 5,000 cap, average sections
  were still led by `entities` at 143,557 B, followed by
  `minimapEntities` 28,350 B, `projectiles` 26,873 B, and
  `audioEvents` 21,555 B.

Why it matters:
Snapshot bandwidth is per remote client and burns host upstream first.
Even when the 200-unit delta stream is under target and the high-count
FULLSNAP bootstrap is under 1 MiB, every remote client multiplies the
DIFFSNAP stream. The 1,000 / 5,000-unit rows still exceed the normal
internet delta budget by several multiples.

Fix direction:
Do child items as separate, measurable PRs. FULLSNAP/static sections are
not the active blocker; the next work is SNAP-WIRE-01A.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SNAP-WIRE-01A - child: high-count DIFFSNAP rows exceed the 64 KiB avg /
                128 KiB hi targets after the packed-row passes.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Evidence:
- Captured PLAYER CLIENT matrix, 2026-05-22, keyframes disabled, fog
  disabled, debug grid disabled, all demo unit types enabled, base
  position threshold at 5, base rotation-position threshold at 0.1,
  and owned/allied/observed threshold multipliers at 1x:
  - cap 1,000: DS avg 159,039-211,790 B, DS hi 218,564-244,394 B.
  - cap 5,000: DS avg 671,714-770,596 B, DS hi 841,268-859,546 B.
  - FULLSNAP bootstrap stayed under target: about 389-392 KiB at cap
    1,000 and 918-923 KiB at cap 5,000.
- A representative max-delta section breakdown from the same harness
  family showed `entities` as the dominant section at high count:
  roughly 614 KiB / 72% of an 849 KiB cap-5,000 delta. Projectiles,
  audio events, and minimap entities followed at roughly 103 KiB,
  70 KiB, and 62 KiB respectively.
- After the version-3 unit turret slab and continuous audio lifecycle
  pass, the 2026-05-22 Vite SSR probe measured cap 1,000 / 10 SPS at
  43,519 B avg / 83,281 B hi, which is inside the normal 64 KiB avg /
  128 KiB hi targets. Cap 5,000 / 10 SPS improved to 221,055 B avg /
  322,977 B hi but remains over budget; average packed sections were
  `entities` 143,557 B, `minimapEntities` 28,350 B, `projectiles`
  26,873 B, and `audioEvents` 21,555 B.

Why it matters:
The remaining bandwidth problem is steady-state upstream, not rare
bootstrap recovery. At 5,000 active units a single remote client can
consume several megabits/sec even before WebRTC framing, retransmits,
or additional remote clients multiply the stream.

Fix direction:
Continue with the high-count entity delta shape. The 1,000-cap row is
now inside target, but the 5,000-cap row is still entity-led. Prefer
denser movement/turret slabs, lower-detail high-count entity cadence, or
the existing Rust entity-row encoder shape over nested per-entity arrays
where that reduces bytes and keeps a single source of truth. After the
5,000-cap entity stream is reduced, rerun the same matrix and then
budget the next largest delta sections: projectile/beam bursts,
minimap rows, and remaining audio-event bursts.
