 ISSUES:

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 FOG OF WAR — design vs. current implementation
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

How fog of war should work in an RTS (the canonical model, à la Total Annihilation /
Supreme Commander, Starcraft, Age of Empires):

Every map cell, from a given player's POV, exists in one of THREE states:

  1. UNEXPLORED. Never seen by any of that player's vision sources.
     Rendered as solid black/heavy fog. Terrain unknown, no entities shown.

  2. EXPLORED-BUT-DARK ("shroud" or "soft fog"). Seen at some point, but not
     currently in vision. Rendered as a dimmed/desaturated overlay.
       - Static features (terrain, neutral resources, ENEMY BUILDINGS): shown as
         their LAST-SEEN snapshot — the building stays as a "ghost" at the
         position the player last observed it. If it has since been destroyed
         or moved, the player only finds out by re-visiting.
       - Mobile units (enemy units): NOT shown. They could have moved, so
         showing a ghost would be a lie. Once a player loses LOS on an enemy
         unit, it disappears.

  3. CURRENTLY VISIBLE. At least one of the player's units/buildings has line
     of sight to this tile right now. Real-time information; all entities and
     their state are shown accurately.

Unit sight (line of sight, LOS):
  - Each entity has a sight radius — usually a circle around the entity.
  - Scout-type units typically have larger radii than line units.
  - Buildings have a smaller "passive" radius.
  - Vision is BLOCKED by terrain features in many RTS games (high ground,
     forests, structures). A unit on the far side of a mountain shouldn't
     be visible just because it's inside the circle.
  - Day/night cycles or weather can reduce sight radius (some games).
  - Vision is shared between allies (team play).

Radar (and the related "sonar" / "scanner"):
  - Larger radius than unit sight, but provides STRICTLY LESS information
     than direct vision: it shows enemies as blips on the minimap (and in
     some games, as generic dots in the world) without identifying the unit
     type, owner-specific state, HP, orders, etc.
  - Radar does NOT lift the fog-of-war shroud — visited tiles stay
     "explored-but-dark," radar just adds a contemporaneous blip overlay.
  - Stealth/cloaked units do not appear on radar. Radar jamming hides
     friendly units from enemy radar. (Standard counters.)
  - Air units may need air radar (separate channel) in some games.

Detection (counter to stealth):
  - Cloaked/stealth units are invisible to normal vision.
  - Specific detector units/buildings reveal cloak within their detect
     radius. Without a detector, cloaked enemies are blind spots — even
     while standing in your camp.

Sound / audio reveal:
  - Many RTS games play unit sounds even from out-of-vision positions when
     close enough, hinting at enemy presence without revealing position.

Initial reveal:
  - At game start, the area around each player's starting base is already
     in CURRENTLY VISIBLE. The rest of the map is UNEXPLORED.

What our project currently has:

  - One boolean: world.fogOfWarEnabled. When on, the per-recipient snapshot
     is filtered through SnapshotVisibility (point-in-circle, with a spatial
     hash grid).
  - Vision sources: a player's own UNITS (1200), COMMANDERS (1600), buildings
     (1000), RADAR building (4200), with extra range pads for turret reach
     and builder reach. All sources contribute to the same merged vision
     mask — radar is just "a building with a bigger circle."
  - Entity-private fields (HP, action state, orders) stripped for enemies.
  - When an enemy entity leaves the player's vision, the server emits an
     explicit removal so the client DELETES the entity. Re-entering vision
     re-sends it as a full snapshot.
  - Audio events and projectile spawns are gated by isPointVisible(spawn pos).
  - The dark canvas-alpha overlay that used to dim explored areas was
     removed when "keep enemies as ghosts" was scrapped.

Mismatches with the canonical model (each a discrete issue below):

[FOW-01] No three-state shroud. The canvas does not distinguish UNEXPLORED
  from EXPLORED-BUT-DARK from CURRENTLY VISIBLE. After the overlay was
  removed there is NO visual indication of explored vs. unexplored
  terrain at all — the player just sees the bare ground/sky everywhere.

[FOW-02] No last-seen ghosts for enemy BUILDINGS. Standard RTS keeps
  enemy buildings at their last-observed position once you've seen them,
  since buildings rarely move. Current behavior drops them the moment
  they exit vision, which is correct for mobile units but wrong for
  static structures. (The user explicitly asked for this drop behavior
  for UNITS — but the same change also wiped building memory, which is
  a regression vs. typical RTS expectations.)

[FOW-03] Radar is "just a bigger vision circle." Radar should provide a
  WEAKER form of awareness (blips/positions only, no unit identification,
  no HP, no orders) at a LARGER radius — not a strict superset of direct
  vision. As written, a radar building reveals enemies in full fidelity
  the same as a commander.

[FOW-04] No line-of-sight blocking by terrain. A unit on the far side
  of a hill, behind a forest, or beyond a wall is visible if it falls
  within the circle. The world has a heightmap (Terrain) but the
  visibility test is purely 2D point-in-circle.

[FOW-05] No stealth/cloak mechanic and therefore no detector mechanic.
  Every unit is visible to anyone with overlapping vision. Adding cloak
  later will require new entity flags and a "detector radius" field
  separate from vision radius.

[FOW-06] No team / shared vision. SnapshotVisibility.forRecipient takes a
  single PlayerId. If allies are added, each ally needs its own filter
  pass OR the visibility mask needs to union all allied players. The
  current code is structurally per-player only.

[FOW-07] Spectators have no proper view. A spectator (no recipientPlayerId)
  currently bypasses fog filtering entirely (sees everything), which is
  acceptable for an admin/observer but means there is no path to "spectate
  as if you were Player N" for casting / replays.

[FOW-08] Phantom damage from out-of-vision attackers. A projectile fired
  by an invisible enemy is filtered out at spawn (shouldSendProjectileSpawnEvent
  checks the spawn point). The simulation still resolves the damage
  authoritatively, so the player's unit takes HP loss with no visible
  projectile and no visible source. Standard RTS shows the impact + a
  blood/spark effect and often briefly reveals the attacker (Starcraft's
  "ping the source" behavior).

[FOW-09] Audio reveal is binary on vision. Currently a sound event is
  only sent if its origin is in vision. Many RTS games play attenuated
  audio from out-of-vision events to hint at distant fights ("I hear
  shooting from the south but can't see"). Our current setup gives no
  such hint.

[FOW-10] No initial spawn reveal independent of vision. The starting
  area is visible only because the commander stands there and provides
  vision. If a future commander has zero starting vision (e.g., a
  burrowed start), the player would see nothing at all. There is no
  separate "reveal the spawn box at t=0" pass.

[FOW-11] No persistent vision history. The server does not track which
  tiles a player has ever seen. Without this, EXPLORED-BUT-DARK cannot
  be implemented even if the renderer were added back, because there
  is no source of truth for "has player X ever seen tile (cx,cy)?".

[FOW-12] Vision toggling forces a full keyframe per recipient. When the
  host toggles fog mid-game, GameServer.forceNextSnapshotKeyframe makes
  the next snapshot a keyframe for ALL listeners, not just per-recipient.
  Acceptable today (toggle is rare) but worth documenting if/when
  finer-grained reveal mechanics (scans, pings) land.

[FOW-13] Owner-aware filter is correct but undocumented. isEntityVisible
  short-circuits true for owned entities — this is right (you always see
  your own stuff), but the invariant lives only in the function body. If
  team-vision is added later, this becomes "owned OR allied."

[FOW-14] No "scan" / temporary reveal API. There is no command type that
  reveals an arbitrary area for a fixed duration (Starcraft scanner
  sweep, Supreme Commander recon drone). All vision today is tied to
  a long-lived entity.

[FOW-15] Vision radius constants are file-scoped magic numbers. UNIT,
  COMMANDER, BUILDING, RADAR radii live as `export const` at the top
  of stateSerializerVisibility.ts. The radar value (4200) duplicates
  the design intent expressed in DEMO_CONFIG.baseRings.radar but there
  is no single declarative source — changing radar's effective coverage
  means editing both files.

[FOW-16] Buildings under construction provide vision (canEntityProvideVision
  returns hp > 0). A half-built radar with 1 HP currently exposes its
  full 4200 radius. Usually RTS games either gate radar vision on
  "construction complete" or scale it with build progress.

[FOW-17] Dead-but-corpse entities don't appear in the test (hp > 0
  check filters them), but visible entity removals on death only emit
  if the dying entity is currently in vision (shouldSendRemoval). If a
  player saw an enemy unit die just outside their vision, they never
  learn it died — the entity simply vanishes the same way a unit
  leaving vision does. Distinguishing "out-of-sight" from "destroyed"
  matters for player feedback ("did I kill it?").
