 PREDICT serializer — fully-omit-via-optional-wire-fields (deferred)
==============================================================================
The PREDICT POS/VEL/ACC scheme (PLAYER CLIENT bar three-way toggle for
client-side extrapolation between server snapshots) is fully wired
end-to-end. The local + remote bandwidth gate zeros velocity /
acceleration fields on the wire for recipients in POS / VEL mode
(zero encodes in 1-byte MessagePack ints vs ~5-byte quantized floats,
so this is a real wire-byte saving even without making the fields
optional).

One follow-up remains: make velocity / accel wire fields OPTIONAL so
MessagePack drops them entirely instead of shipping zeros. Bigger
saving, much wider blast radius (every reader path needs `?? 0`
defaults). Defer until profiling shows the current zero-write payload
isn't already meeting bandwidth targets.

==============================================================================
 Quaternion + ω-vector + α-vector representation (future feature)
==============================================================================
Don't refactor existing turret yaw+pitch math to quaternion. The
decoupled-Euler scalar springs are already physically correct for
tank-style two-motor turrets:
  - Same k and c on both axes → under critical damping, yaw and pitch
    errors decay proportionally and the (yaw, pitch) Euler-space
    trajectory is a straight line from initial to target.
  - Real tank turrets have two independent motors (yaw ring + pitch
    trunnion); the decoupled scalar springs are a faithful model of
    that mechanism. A quaternion shortest-path geodesic spring models
    a single 3-DOF free gimbal instead — less physical for tanks, not
    more.

Where quaternion + ω-vector + α-vector representation actually buys
something is FUTURE content that needs roll or arbitrary orientation:
  - Hovercraft banking into a turn
  - Spinning projectiles around the flight axis
  - Ragdoll debris, ships listing in waves, etc.

When such an entity is added, build it on quaternions from day one.
PREDICT POS/VEL/ACC semantics fall out naturally from that
representation (single quat integrate; one ω·dt step; one α·dt step)
without needing a per-axis scalar bundle on the wire.

Until such a feature ships, no quaternion refactor is queued.
