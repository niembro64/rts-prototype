The "tick" unit legs used to be stretched downwards while in the air, which made the landing seem logical, normally when animals land they land with extended legs that can compress over time, absorbing the impact. The current tick legs are pulled up to their body while in the air. Look up how animals actually have their legs positioned when jumping, while in the air, and then when landing, and adjust the tick legs to match that. This will make the jump and landing look more natural. We may need a more advanced leg extension system to handle how animals actually do it, where the amount the legs are stretched or pulled up depends on distance from the ground?
